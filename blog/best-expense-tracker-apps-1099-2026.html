Frequently Asked Questions — Expense Trackers for 1099 Workers

What is the best free expense tracker for 1099 workers in 2026?

Stride is the best free option. It automatically tracks mileage at $0.725/mile, logs expenses with photo receipts, and generates IRS-ready annual reports — all at no cost with no premium tier.

Is Everlance worth paying for in 2026?

Yes, if you drive more than 500 miles per month. Everlance Premium ($60/year) pays for itself with just 83 extra miles tracked. The automatic classification and quarterly tax estimates are worth the cost for frequent drivers.

Can I use QuickBooks Self-Employed for 1099 taxes?

Yes. QuickBooks Self-Employed ($15/month) tracks mileage, estimates quarterly taxes, and exports directly to TurboTax Self-Employed. It is the best option if you already use TurboTax for filing.

How much can expense tracking save a 1099 worker?

On 10,000 miles driven, the mileage deduction is $7,250 at $0.725/mile. In the 22% tax bracket that saves $1,595 in federal taxes. Missing expenses can easily cost $2,000+ per year in unnecessary taxes.

Does the IRS require a mileage log for 1099 workers?

Yes. The IRS requires a contemporaneous mileage log showing date, destination, business purpose, and miles driven for each trip. Apps like Stride and Everlance generate IRS-compliant reports automatically.

What expenses can 1099 gig workers track?

Mileage ($0.725/mile), phone and data plan (business %), car insurance (business %), maintenance and repairs, parking and tolls, equipment, health insurance premiums, and 50% of self-employment tax paid.

Is Wave a good option for 1099 freelancers?

Wave is best for freelancers with multiple clients who need free invoicing and accounting. For delivery and rideshare drivers, the lack of automatic mileage tracking makes it less suitable — use Stride instead.
