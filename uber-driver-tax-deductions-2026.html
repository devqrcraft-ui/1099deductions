Uber Driver Tax Deductions 2026 — Complete Rideshare Write-Off Checklist

Uber drivers are independent contractors who receive a 1099-NEC at year end. Uber withholds zero taxes — but you can deduct a wide range of business expenses to reduce your self-employment tax and income tax. This guide lists every IRS Schedule C deduction available to Uber rideshare drivers in 2026.

  • All receipts for car washes, supplies, phone bill, insurance

Uber Driver 1099 Tax FAQ

Does Uber withhold taxes from my payments?
No. Uber classifies drivers as independent contractors. No federal, state, or FICA taxes are withheld. You must pay quarterly estimated taxes using IRS Form 1040-ES.
What 1099 does Uber send in 2026?
Uber sends a 1099-NEC if you earn $600+ in non-ride income (bonuses, incentives) or a 1099-K if you process $5,000+ in payments. You must report all income regardless.
Can I deduct mileage AND actual car expenses?
No — choose one method per vehicle per year. Standard mileage ($0.725/mile) is simpler. Actual expense method requires tracking gas, repairs, insurance, and depreciation.
What is the SE tax rate for Uber drivers in 2026?
Self-employment tax is 15.3% on 92.35% of net profit. This covers Social Security (12.4%) and Medicare (2.9%). You deduct 50% of SE tax paid from your gross income.
Do I need to pay Uber quarterly taxes?
Yes, if you expect to owe $1,000+ in federal taxes. 2026 due dates: April 15, June 16, September 15, January 15 2027.
