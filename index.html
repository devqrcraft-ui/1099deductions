Common 1099 Tax Deductions for Gig Workers
These 7 categories apply to most self-employed and 1099 workers. Select your job above for a complete checklist.
Vehicle & Mileage
$0.725/mile standard rate (2026) or actual vehicle expenses. Largest deduction for most delivery and rideshare workers.
Home Office
$5/sq ft up to 300 sq ft ($1,500 max) simplified method, or actual expenses. Must be used regularly and exclusively for business.
Phone & Internet
Business-use percentage of your phone and data plan. Typically 50-80% for gig workers who use phone for navigation and orders.
Software & Apps
Accounting software (QuickBooks, Wave), mileage tracking apps (MileIQ), and any paid tools used to manage your gig business.
Health Insurance
100% of premiums deductible if not eligible for employer coverage. Taken above-the-line on Schedule 1, not Schedule C.
SE Tax Deduction
Deduct 50% of self-employment tax paid (Schedule SE). Reduces adjusted gross income - not on Schedule C but saves real money.
Equipment & Supplies
Insulated bags, cameras, tools, and gear required for your specific gig job. Must be ordinary and necessary for your trade.
Frequently Asked Questions
What deductions can all gig workers claim in 2026?
Mileage ($0.725/mile for driving jobs), phone and data plan (business %), home office (up to $1,500/year), health insurance premiums, and equipment required for the job.
What is self-employment tax and how much is it?
SE tax is 15.3% on net profit (Social Security 12.4% + Medicare 2.9%). You can deduct half of SE tax paid on your Form 1040. Deductions reduce net profit and therefore SE tax.
Do gig workers need to pay quarterly taxes in 2026?
If you expect to owe $1,000+ in federal tax for the year, you must pay quarterly. Deadlines: April 15, June 16, September 15, January 15, 2027.
Can gig workers deduct health insurance?
Yes. If you are not eligible for employer-sponsored health insurance, you can deduct 100% of your health insurance premiums as an above-the-line deduction on Schedule 1.
What records should gig workers keep for taxes?
Mileage log (if driving), all receipts for business expenses, bank statements, 1099 forms, and quarterly payment records. Keep for at least 3 years.
