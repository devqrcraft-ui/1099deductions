Frequently Asked Questions
What deductions can all gig workers claim in 2026?
Mileage ($0.70/mile for driving jobs), phone and data plan (business %), home office (up to $1,500/year), health insurance premiums, and equipment required for the job.
What is self-employment tax and how much is it?
SE tax is 15.3% on net profit (Social Security 12.4% + Medicare 2.9%). You can deduct half of SE tax paid on your Form 1040. Deductions reduce net profit and therefore SE tax.
Do gig workers need to pay quarterly taxes in 2026?
If you expect to owe $1,000+ in federal tax for the year, you must pay quarterly. Deadlines: April 15, June 16, September 15, January 15, 2027.
Can gig workers deduct health insurance?
Yes. If you are not eligible for employer-sponsored health insurance, you can deduct 100% of your health insurance premiums as an above-the-line deduction on Schedule 1.
What records should gig workers keep for taxes?
Mileage log (if driving), all receipts for business expenses, bank statements, 1099 forms, and quarterly payment records. Keep for at least 3 years.
