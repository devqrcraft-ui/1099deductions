1099 Tax Deductions List 2026 —
Top Write-Offs for Contractors & Freelancers

Every self-employed tax write-off available to 1099 workers in 2026 — organized by category. Whether you drive for DoorDash, sell on Etsy, create on OnlyFans, or freelance on Upwork, these deductions reduce your IRS Schedule C taxable income and lower your self-employment tax. All amounts reflect 2026 IRS rules. Not tax advice — consult a CPA for your specific situation.

💡 How to use this list: Every deduction below goes on IRS Schedule C (Profit or Loss from Business). The deduction reduces your net profit, which lowers both your income tax and your 15.3% self-employment tax. Keep receipts and records for at least 3 years.

🚗 Vehicle & Mileage

  • ✓Standard Mileage Rate — $0.725 per business mile in 2026. Track every trip with an app (MileIQ, Everlance). Covers fuel, depreciation, insurance implicitly.
  • ✓Actual Vehicle Expenses — Alternative to mileage: deduct actual gas, oil, repairs, insurance, registration — proportional to business use %
  • ✓Parking & Tolls — 100% deductible even if using standard mileage method. Keep receipts per trip.
  • ✓Car Washes — For vehicles used for business deliveries or rideshare. Deduct business-use %.

📱 Phone & Internet

  • ✓Cell Phone Bill — Deduct the business-use percentage (typically 50–80%). Must document business vs personal use.
  • ✓Internet Bill — For creators, freelancers, remote workers — deduct 80–100% if used primarily for work.
  • ✓Phone Accessories — GPS mounts, chargers, dashcams used for business. 100% deductible.

🏠 Home Office

  • ✓Simplified Method — $5/sq ft × up to 300 sq ft = max $1,500/year. Space must be used exclusively for business.
  • ✓Actual Expense Method — Deduct proportion of rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance based on office sq ft / total home sq ft.
  • ✓Home Office Furniture — Desk, chair, shelving used exclusively in home office. Deductible.

💊 Health & Insurance

  • ✓Health Insurance Premiums — 100% deductible if not eligible for employer coverage. Above-the-line deduction on Schedule 1.
  • ✓Dental & Vision Insurance — Also deductible as part of self-employed health insurance deduction.
  • ✓Business Insurance — Liability insurance, professional indemnity insurance. 100% deductible.

💻 Equipment & Supplies

  • ✓Computers & Hardware — Section 179 allows 100% deduction in year of purchase. Or depreciate over 5 years.
  • ✓Cameras & Video Gear — For creators, photographers. 100% deductible if used exclusively for business.
  • ✓Tools & Equipment — Handymen, tradespeople. 100% deductible under Section 179.
  • ✓Office Supplies — Paper, ink, pens, postage. 100% deductible.

📊 Software & Subscriptions

  • ✓Software Subscriptions — Adobe, Figma, Slack, Notion, QuickBooks, etc. 100% deductible.
  • ✓AI Tools — ChatGPT Plus, Claude, Midjourney if used for business. 100% deductible.
  • ✓Mileage Tracking Apps — MileIQ, Everlance subscriptions. 100% deductible.

📚 Professional Development

  • ✓Online Courses — Directly related to your current business (not for new career). 100% deductible.
  • ✓Books & Publications — Industry books, trade magazines, research subscriptions.
  • ✓Professional Memberships — Industry associations, trade organizations.

💼 Platform & Professional Fees

  • ✓Platform Fees — OnlyFans 20%, Upwork 5–20%, Fiverr 20%, Turo 15–35% — all 100% deductible.
  • ✓Tax Preparation — Cost of tax software (TurboTax, TaxAct) or CPA fees. 100% deductible.
  • ✓Legal & Accounting Fees — Attorney fees and bookkeeping costs for your business.

📋 SE Tax Deduction

  • ✓50% SE Tax Deduction — Deduct half of self-employment tax paid on Form 1040 Schedule 1. Automatically calculated.
  • ✓QBI Deduction — Qualified Business Income deduction — up to 20% of net business income for eligible filers.

What 1099 Workers Cannot Deduct

  • ✗Personal meals — Meals during your workday are not deductible unless it is a genuine business meeting
  • ✗Traffic fines & penalties — IRS explicitly disallows deducting fines and penalties
  • ✗Personal commuting — Miles from home to your first job location are personal, not business
  • ✗Personal clothing — Only deductible if it is a uniform not suitable for everyday wear
  • ✗Home expenses (non-office) — Only the portion used exclusively for business qualifies

Record-Keeping Essentials

📓 Mileage Log
Date, destination, business purpose, miles driven. Apps: MileIQ, Everlance, Stride.
🧾 Receipts
All business purchases. Photo with your phone immediately after purchase.
🏦 Bank Statements
Separate business account makes tracking easier and audit-proof.
📄 1099 Forms
Keep all 1099-NEC and 1099-K forms received. Report ALL income even without a 1099.
📅 Quarterly Records
Proof of estimated tax payments (IRS EFTPS confirmation).
⏱️ 3-Year Rule
IRS can audit up to 3 years back. Keep all records at least that long.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 2026 IRS standard mileage rate?
The 2026 IRS standard mileage rate is $0.725 per mile for business use. Gig workers who drive for DoorDash, Uber, Lyft, Instacart or Amazon Flex can use this rate instead of tracking actual vehicle expenses.
Can self-employed workers deduct health insurance?
Yes. Self-employed individuals not eligible for employer-sponsored health insurance can deduct 100% of health insurance premiums as an above-the-line deduction on Schedule 1 of Form 1040.
What is the home office deduction for 2026?
The simplified method allows $5 per square foot of dedicated workspace, up to 300 square feet, for a maximum of $1,500 per year. The space must be used regularly and exclusively for business.
What records do I need for 1099 tax deductions?
Keep a mileage log, receipts for all business expenses, bank statements, 1099 forms, and quarterly payment records. The IRS recommends keeping records for at least 3 years.
