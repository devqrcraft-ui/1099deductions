Why 1099 and W-2 Taxes Are Different

W-2 Employee

Your employer withholds taxes and pays half your FICA (7.65%). You never see the employer half — but on a $75,000 salary it costs them ~$5,750 extra.

  • FICA: you pay 7.65%
  • Employer pays other 7.65%
  • Federal and state withheld automatically
  • Benefits (health, 401k) often included

1099 Contractor

You pay both halves of FICA as SE tax (15.3%), but can deduct business expenses, half of SE tax, and up to 20% QBI deduction — often closing the gap significantly.

  • SE tax: 15.3% on 92.35% of net income
  • Deduct half SE tax from AGI
  • QBI deduction: up to 20%
  • All business expenses: Schedule C

Frequently Asked Questions

Do 1099 workers pay more taxes than W-2 employees?
1099 contractors pay SE tax of 15.3% vs 7.65% FICA for W-2 workers. However, deductions (half SE tax, QBI, Schedule C expenses) often bring the effective rate close to or below W-2 taxes. The more legitimate expenses you deduct, the more favorable 1099 becomes.
What is self-employment tax in 2026?
SE tax is 15.3% on the first $176,100 of net self-employment income (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare), applied to 92.35% of net earnings. Above $176,100 only the 2.9% Medicare rate applies. You deduct half of SE tax from gross income before calculating federal tax.
What is the QBI deduction and who qualifies?
The Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction lets eligible self-employed workers deduct up to 20% of qualified business income. Most gig workers qualify if income is below $197,300 (single) or $394,600 (MFJ) in 2026. Certain professional service businesses have additional limits.
Should I form an S-Corp to reduce SE tax?
High earners (typically $80,000+ net self-employment income) may save SE tax via S-Corp election and a reasonable salary. At lower income levels the administrative costs usually outweigh any savings. Consult a licensed CPA before making this decision.
