Uber Driver Tax Deductions 2026

Last updated: June 2026  ·  By Ethan Blake  ·  ~6 min read · 1,600 words
2026 Tax Summary — Uber Driver

On $35,000 net income: SE tax ~$7,750. Quarterly payment: $1,938. Set aside 22% of every payment. Track every mile at 72.5¢ — 10,000 miles saves $1,087 in federal tax alone.

Uber drivers are independent contractors. You receive a 1099-K or 1099-NEC and file Schedule C. Every mile driven with the Uber app on — including deadhead miles between rides — is deductible at $0.725 per mile for 2026.

Quick answer: Uber drivers can deduct mileage at $0.725/mile, phone bills, car insurance, maintenance, dash cam, tolls, parking, and 50% of self-employment tax. The SE tax deduction itself reduces your adjusted gross income — not just taxable income.

What Can Uber Drivers Deduct in 2026?

Answer

Uber drivers can deduct any ordinary and necessary business expense. The biggest deductions are mileage ($0.725/mile), phone (business %), car insurance (business %), maintenance, and 50% of SE tax. You cannot deduct personal commuting miles or non-business expenses.

Mileage$0.725/mile

All miles driven with the Uber app on — to pick up, during ride, and between rides. Keep a mileage log or use an app like Stride or Everlance.

Phone & Data PlanBusiness %

Deduct the percentage of your phone bill used for Uber navigation, communication with riders, and the app. Most drivers claim 60–80%.

Car InsuranceBusiness %

Deduct the business-use portion of your personal auto insurance. If you use actual expense method — deduct full rideshare policy cost.

Car Washes100%

Car washes and interior cleaning are fully deductible as vehicle maintenance directly required by your Uber business.

Dash Cam100%

A dash cam used exclusively for Uber driving is 100% deductible as a business safety device. Keep your purchase receipt.

Tolls & Parking100%

All tolls and parking fees incurred while driving for Uber are fully deductible — even if using the standard mileage method.

Bottled Water / Snacks100%

Amenities provided to riders — water, mints, phone chargers — are deductible supplies. Keep receipts and note "rider amenities" on them.

Health Insurance100% if eligible

Self-employed health insurance premiums are deductible above the line on Form 1040 if you are not eligible for employer-sponsored coverage.

50% SE TaxAbove the line

Half of your self-employment tax paid is deductible on Form 1040 Schedule 1. This reduces your AGI — not just itemized deductions.

Mileage vs. Actual Expense Method — Which Is Better?

Answer

Most Uber drivers benefit more from the standard mileage rate ($0.725/mile) because it is simpler and covers depreciation automatically. The actual expense method is better for expensive vehicles with high insurance, gas, and repair costs — calculate both each year and take the larger deduction.

MethodBest ForRecord KeepingEstimated Deduction (15k miles)
Standard Mileage ($0.725/mi)Most driversMileage log only$10,875
Actual ExpensesHigh-cost vehiclesAll receipts requiredVaries — $8,000–$14,000
⚠
IRS Rule: Choose at the Start If you use the actual expense method in year 1, you cannot switch to standard mileage in later years for that vehicle. If you start with standard mileage, you can switch to actual later.

Answer

Uber drivers who expect to owe $1,000+ in taxes must pay estimated taxes quarterly. Missing a deadline triggers an IRS underpayment penalty — currently 8% annualized. Pay via IRS Direct Pay or mail Form 1040-ES.

Frequently Asked Questions — Uber Driver Taxes 2026

What can Uber drivers deduct on taxes in 2026?

Mileage at $0.725/mile, phone and data (business %), car insurance (business %), maintenance and repairs, car washes, dash cam, tolls, parking, rider amenities, and 50% of self-employment tax paid.

How much tax does an Uber driver pay in 2026?

On $35,000 net income: SE tax is approximately $7,750. Quarterly estimated payment is $1,938. Set aside 22% of each payment. Add your state income tax on top of federal.

Do Uber drivers pay quarterly taxes?

Yes. If you expect to owe $1,000+ at filing, you must pay quarterly estimated taxes using IRS Form 1040-ES. Deadlines: April 15, June 16, September 15, January 15.

Is Uber mileage fully deductible in 2026?

Yes. All miles driven with the Uber app on — including driving to pick up a rider and returning from a drop-off — are deductible at $0.725 per mile for 2026.

Can Uber drivers deduct car payments?

Not directly. You deduct either standard mileage ($0.725/mile) or actual expenses including depreciation. Most drivers benefit more from the mileage method — it already accounts for vehicle wear.

What is the 1099-NEC threshold for Uber in 2026?

Under the OBBBA 2026 the federal 1099-NEC threshold increased to $2,000. However you must still report all self-employment income over $400 net profit on Schedule C regardless of whether you receive a 1099.
