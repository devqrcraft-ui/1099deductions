Frequently Asked Questions
“Self-employed individuals must pay both the employee and employer portions of Social Security and Medicare taxes, totaling 15.3% on net earnings up to $176,100.” — IRS.gov — Self-Employed Tax Center
How much tax does a Uber Eats worker pay in 2026?
On $35 000 net income: approximately $4 944 SE tax (15.3%) + federal income tax = $7 750 total estimated. Set aside 25-30% of every payment.
What is the mileage deduction for Uber Eats in 2026?
The 2026 IRS standard mileage rate is 72.5 cents per mile. Track every business mile — 15,000 miles = $10,875 deduction. This is typically the largest write-off.
Does Uber Eats withhold taxes?
No. Uber Eats pays independent contractors via 1099-NEC (threshold $2,000 in 2026 under OBBBA). You must pay SE tax and income tax yourself via quarterly estimated payments.
When are quarterly tax deadlines for Uber Eats workers in 2026?
Q1: April 15, Q2: June 16, Q3: September 15, Q4: January 15, 2027. Missing a deadline triggers IRS underpayment penalty.
What is the QBI deduction for Uber Eats workers?
Self-employed Uber Eats workers can deduct 20% of net profit under Section 199A (QBI deduction). On $35 000 net: $7 000 deduction.
