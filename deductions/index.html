Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 1099 worker and what deductions can they claim?
A 1099 worker is a self-employed independent contractor who receives a 1099-NEC or 1099-K instead of a W-2. They can deduct all ordinary and necessary business expenses on IRS Schedule C, including mileage, phone, home office, supplies, and platform fees.
What is IRS Schedule C?
Schedule C (Profit or Loss from Business) is the IRS form where self-employed workers report income and deductible business expenses. Every deduction on this site is Schedule C compliant for the 2026 tax year.
Do gig workers pay self-employment tax?
Yes. Gig workers pay self-employment tax of 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare) on net profit. You can deduct 50% of SE tax paid on Form 1040, and all business deductions reduce your net profit, lowering SE tax.
