❓ Frequently Asked Questions — Freelance Handyman Taxes 2026

Q: Can handymen deduct tools bought at Home Depot?
Yes — hand tools, power tools, drill bits, and other equipment purchased for your handyman business are fully deductible under Section 179 in the year of purchase.
Q: Can I deduct my truck as a handyman?
Yes — if your truck is used for your handyman business, deduct either the standard mileage rate ($0.725/mile) or actual costs (fuel, repairs, insurance, depreciation). Keep a mileage log.
Q: Are work boots and safety gear deductible?
Yes — steel-toed boots, hard hats, safety glasses, gloves, and any safety equipment required for your work are deductible. Regular clothing that can be worn outside work is not deductible.
Q: Do I need a Schedule C as a self-employed handyman?
Yes. You must file Schedule C with your Form 1040 to report business income and deductions. You also owe 15.3% self-employment tax on net profit.
Q: Can I deduct materials I buy for a specific job?
Yes — job materials are a direct expense on Schedule C. Keep receipts from Home Depot, Lowe's, and other suppliers. Unbilled or leftover materials at year-end may need to be treated as inventory.
