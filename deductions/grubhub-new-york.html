🍔 Grubhub Tax Deductions in New York 2026

Grubhub drivers are independent contractors who receive a 1099-NEC. Every delivery mile is deductible at the 2026 IRS standard mileage rate of $0.725/mile. This page covers all federal deductions plus New York-specific tax rules for food delivery workers.

New York State Tax: 4%–10.9%
New York has state income tax rates from 4% to 10.9%. NYC residents also pay city income tax (3.08%–3.876%) on top of state tax.
New York Note for Grubhub Workers New York City residents pay both state AND city income tax — the combined burden can reach 14.8% on top of federal taxes.

New York Grubhub Tax FAQs

What can New York Grubhub drivers deduct in 2026?
Mileage ($0.725/mile), insulated bags, phone, car maintenance, parking, and NYC tolls (all 100% deductible during active deliveries). NYC congestion pricing fees during deliveries are also deductible.
Does NYC congestion pricing affect Grubhub driver deductions?
NYC congestion pricing fees paid while on active Grubhub deliveries are 100% deductible as a business expense. Keep records of each charge.
Do New York Grubhub drivers pay city income tax?
NYC residents pay city income tax (3.08%–3.876%) on net profit plus state income tax (4%–10.9%). All deductions reduce both state and city taxable income.
Do New York Grubhub drivers pay quarterly to NYS?
Yes. Quarterly to both IRS and NY State Department of Taxation. Q1 deadline April 15 for both.
