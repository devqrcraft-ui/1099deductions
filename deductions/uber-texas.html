Frequently Asked Questions
Do Texas Uber drivers pay state income tax?
No. Texas has no state income tax. You only pay federal income tax and self-employment tax (15.3%). This saves Texas gig workers thousands vs. California drivers.
What can Texas Uber drivers deduct in 2026?
Mileage ($0.70/mile), phone and data, vehicle maintenance, parking, tolls, dash cam, phone mount, and car washes proportional to business use.
Do Texas Uber drivers pay quarterly estimated taxes?
Yes, federal quarterly taxes are still required (Texas has no state income tax). Deadlines: April 15, June 16, September 15, January 15, 2027.
What is the biggest tax deduction for Texas Uber drivers?
Standard mileage ($0.70/mile) is usually the largest. At 20,000 miles/year that is $13,400 in deductions, directly reducing your taxable income.
Can Texas Uber drivers deduct toll fees on I-35 or other highways?
Yes. All tolls incurred while driving for Uber are 100% deductible business expenses.
