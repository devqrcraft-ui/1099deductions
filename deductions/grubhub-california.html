🍔 Grubhub Tax Deductions in California 2026

Grubhub drivers are independent contractors who receive a 1099-NEC. Every delivery mile is deductible at the 2026 IRS standard mileage rate of $0.67/mile. This page covers all federal deductions plus California-specific tax rules for food delivery workers.

California has the highest state income tax in the US, up to 13.3%. Gig workers pay state income tax on net profit after all federal deductions.

California Grubhub Tax FAQs

What can California Grubhub drivers deduct in 2026?
Mileage ($0.67/mile), insulated bags, phone, car maintenance, parking, and tolls. Grubhub drivers in California operate as independent contractors under Prop 22.
Are California Grubhub drivers contractors or employees?
Under Prop 22, app-based food delivery drivers in California are independent contractors. You file Schedule C and are responsible for SE tax and state income tax.
Do California Grubhub drivers pay quarterly to FTB?
Yes. Quarterly to both IRS and California FTB. Q1 deadline April 15 for both.
What is the state tax rate for California Grubhub drivers?
California taxes net profit at 1%–13.3%. Most delivery drivers earning $30,000–$60,000 net pay an effective state rate of 4–8%.
