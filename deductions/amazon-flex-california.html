📦 Amazon Flex Tax Deductions in California 2026

Amazon Flex drivers are independent contractors who use their own vehicles to deliver packages. Every delivery mile and required equipment is deductible. This page covers all federal deductions plus California-specific tax rules for package delivery workers.

California Amazon Flex Tax FAQs

What can California Amazon Flex drivers deduct in 2026?
Mileage ($0.725/mile), cargo organizer, phone, vehicle maintenance, parking, and tolls. California state income tax up to 13.3% applies to net profit after deductions.
Do California Amazon Flex drivers pay quarterly to FTB?
Yes. Quarterly to both IRS and California FTB. Q1 deadline April 15 for both. California safe harbor: pay at least 100% of prior year tax to avoid penalty.
What is the state tax rate for California Amazon Flex drivers?
California taxes net profit at 1%–13.3%. Flex drivers earning $40,000–$70,000 net typically pay an effective state rate of 6–9%.
Can California Amazon Flex drivers deduct cargo equipment?
Yes. Trunk liners, cargo nets, and package organizers are required equipment for the job and are 100% deductible on Schedule C.
