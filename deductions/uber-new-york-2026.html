2026 Tax Summary — Uber New York

New York Uber drivers are independent contractors who file Schedule C. NYC drivers face the highest combined tax burden of any US city — NY state tax up to 10.9% plus the NYC surcharge of 3.876%. The mileage deduction at $0.725/mile and TLC fee deductions are critical to reducing your bill. This page covers all federal and NY-specific deductions for 2026.

What Can New York Uber Drivers Deduct in 2026?

All deductions below are claimed on IRS Schedule C. New York state taxes the net profit remaining after all federal deductions. NYC drivers have additional unique deductions not available in other states.

Mileage Deduction
$0.725/mile
2026 IRS standard rate. Covers fuel, depreciation, oil, tires. Track every mile — deadhead miles to pickup included.
TLC License Fees
100%
NYC TLC For-Hire Vehicle license renewal, drug screening fees, medical exams, and vehicle inspection fees — fully deductible.
Black Car Fund
2.5% of gross
NYC Black Car Fund contributions (2.5% of gross fare) are mandatory for black car and rideshare drivers. Fully deductible on Schedule C.
50% SE Tax Deduction
50% of SE tax
Deduct half of SE tax from federal gross income. On $35K net, saves roughly $370 in federal tax and reduces NY state taxable income.
Phone & Data Plan
Business %
Deduct the percentage of your phone bill used for Uber navigation and communication. 50–80% typical for full-time drivers.
Tolls & Parking
100%
All tolls (bridges, tunnels, E-ZPass) and parking fees while driving for Uber are fully deductible. Keep E-ZPass records.
Car Washes & Cleaning
100%
Required to maintain Uber's vehicle standards. Fully deductible as a business expense when used for rideshare work.
Dash Cam
100%
Safety equipment. Fully deductible in the year of purchase under Section 179. Required by many NYC rideshare insurers.
Passenger Supplies
100%
Water, phone chargers, mints, and air fresheners for riders are ordinary business expenses — fully deductible.

Quarterly Estimated Tax Deadlines for New York Uber Drivers

New York Uber drivers file quarterly with both the IRS and the NY Department of Taxation and Finance. NYC residents must also track the city surcharge liability separately.

  1. April 15, 2026 — Q1 payment to IRS (Form 1040-ES) + NY DTF (Form IT-2105)
  2. June 16, 2026 — Q2 payment to IRS + NY DTF
  3. September 15, 2026 — Q3 payment to IRS + NY DTF
  4. January 15, 2027 — Q4 payment to IRS + NY DTF
  • IRS: pay at IRS Direct Pay using Form 1040-ES
  • NY State: pay at tax.ny.gov using Form IT-2105
  • NYC residents: set aside 30–32% of every Uber payment for all taxes

How to Track Mileage as a New York Uber Driver

At $0.725/mile, 20,000 annual miles = $14,500 deduction — reducing your NY state taxable income by the same amount and saving up to $1,580 in NY state tax alone.

  • Log the date, destination, and business purpose for every trip
  • Include deadhead miles from your home or garage to first pickup
  • Use MileIQ, Stride, or export from the Uber driver app
  • Keep E-ZPass records — all toll receipts are deductible
  • Record odometer on January 1 and December 31 each year

You cannot claim both the standard mileage rate AND actual vehicle expenses for the same vehicle in the same tax year. Most NYC Uber drivers benefit more from the standard mileage rate due to high annual mileage.

New York Uber Driver Tax FAQs 2026

What can New York Uber drivers deduct in 2026?
Mileage at $0.725/mile, TLC license fees, black car fund contributions (2.5%), drug testing, tolls, parking, phone, car washes, dash cam, and passenger supplies. All claimed on Schedule C.
Do New York City Uber drivers pay extra taxes?
Yes. NYC residents pay NY state income tax (4%–10.9%) plus the NYC personal income tax surcharge (3.876% at most income levels). Combined with federal SE tax, total tax can exceed 35% of net income before deductions.
Do New York Uber drivers pay quarterly estimated taxes?
Yes — federal quarterly to IRS (Form 1040-ES) and NY state quarterly to NY DTF (Form IT-2105). Deadlines: Apr 15, Jun 16, Sep 15, Jan 15 2027.
How much tax does a New York Uber driver pay in 2026?
On $35,000 net income: ~$4,950 SE tax + $2,800 federal + $1,540 NY state + $1,358 NYC surcharge = $10,648 total for NYC residents. Mileage deductions significantly reduce this.
Can New York Uber drivers deduct TLC fees?
Yes. TLC license renewal, drug testing, black car fund contributions (2.5% of gross fare), and vehicle inspection fees are all deductible as ordinary business expenses on Schedule C.
