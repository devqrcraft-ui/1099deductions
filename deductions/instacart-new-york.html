🛒 Instacart Tax Deductions in New York 2026

Instacart full-service shoppers are independent contractors who receive a 1099-NEC. You drive your own vehicle to stores and customers — every mile is deductible. This page covers all federal deductions plus New York-specific tax rules for grocery delivery workers.

New York State Tax: 4%–10.9%
New York has state income tax rates from 4% to 10.9%. NYC residents also pay city income tax (3.08%–3.876%) on top of state tax.
New York Note for Instacart Workers New York City residents pay both state AND city income tax — the combined burden can reach 14.8% on top of federal taxes.

New York Instacart Tax FAQs

What can New York Instacart shoppers deduct in 2026?
Mileage ($0.67/mile), insulated bags, phone and data plan, trunk liner, reusable bags, car maintenance, parking, and tolls proportional to delivery miles. NYC bridge/tunnel tolls during active batches are 100% deductible.
Do NYC Instacart shoppers pay city income tax?
Yes. NYC residents pay city income tax (3.08%–3.876%) on net profit in addition to state income tax (4%–10.9%). As a gig worker, all deductions reduce both state and city taxable income.
Do New York Instacart shoppers pay quarterly taxes to NYS?
Yes. Quarterly estimated payments to both IRS and New York State Department of Taxation are required if expected annual tax liability exceeds $1,000.
Can NY Instacart shoppers deduct parking fees?
Yes. Parking fees paid at stores while shopping for Instacart orders are 100% deductible business expenses.
