Uber Tax Deductions in California 2026

2026 Tax Summary — Uber California

Uber drivers pay 15.3% SE tax on net earnings. On $35,000 net income: ~$4,950 SE tax + $2,800 federal + $2,100 California state = $9,850 total tax. Quarterly payment: $2,463. Set aside 28% of every payment. Track every mile at 72.5¢.

Uber drivers in California are independent contractors under Prop 22. You receive a 1099-K and/or 1099-NEC and file Schedule C. Every mile driven for Uber — including deadhead miles to pick up riders — is deductible at $0.725/mile. This page covers all federal deductions plus California-specific rules for 2026.

What Can California Uber Drivers Deduct in 2026?

All deductions below are claimed on IRS Schedule C. California follows federal deduction rules for business expenses — your state tax is calculated on the net profit remaining after all Schedule C deductions.

Mileage Deduction
$0.725/mile
2026 IRS standard rate. Covers fuel, depreciation, oil, tires. Track every mile — app or logbook. Includes deadhead miles to pickup.
50% SE Tax Deduction
50% of SE tax
Deduct half of self-employment tax from federal gross income. On $35K net, saves roughly $370 in federal tax.
Phone & Data Plan
Business %
Deduct the percentage of your phone bill used for Uber navigation, communication, and tracking. 50–80% is typical for full-time drivers.
Car Washes & Cleaning
100%
Required to maintain Uber's cleanliness standards. Fully deductible as a business expense when you drive for Uber.
Tolls & Parking
100%
All tolls and parking fees incurred while driving for Uber are fully deductible. Keep receipts or use the Uber driver app records.
Dash Cam
100%
Safety equipment used exclusively for Uber. Fully deductible in the year of purchase under Section 179 or bonus depreciation.
Passenger Supplies
100%
Water, phone chargers, mints, and air fresheners for riders. Fully deductible as ordinary business expenses.
Vehicle Maintenance
Pro-rata
If using actual expense method instead of mileage: oil changes, tires, repairs proportional to business miles vs. total miles driven.
Home Office
Up to $1,500
Simplified method: $5/sq ft for up to 300 sq ft of dedicated workspace used exclusively for Uber-related admin work.

Quarterly Estimated Tax Deadlines for California Uber Drivers

As a California Uber driver, you file quarterly with both the IRS and the California Franchise Tax Board. Missing a deadline triggers a penalty of 0.5% per month on the underpayment.

  1. April 15, 2026 — Q1 payment (Jan 1 – Mar 31) to IRS + FTB
  2. June 16, 2026 — Q2 payment (Apr 1 – May 31) to IRS + FTB
  3. September 15, 2026 — Q3 payment (Jun 1 – Aug 31) to IRS + FTB
  4. January 15, 2027 — Q4 payment (Sep 1 – Dec 31) to IRS + FTB
  • IRS: pay online at IRS Direct Pay using Form 1040-ES
  • FTB: pay at ftb.ca.gov using Form 540-ES
  • Set aside 25–30% of every Uber payment for taxes

How to Track Mileage as a California Uber Driver

Mileage is the single largest deduction for most Uber drivers. At $0.725/mile, 20,000 annual miles = $14,500 deduction — saving up to $5,500 in taxes. You must keep a contemporaneous log.

  • Log the date, destination, and business purpose for every trip
  • Include deadhead miles from your home to first pickup
  • Use apps like MileIQ, Stride, or the Uber driver app export
  • Record your odometer reading on January 1 and December 31
  • The IRS requires records to be made at or near the time of the trip

You cannot claim both the standard mileage rate AND actual vehicle expenses for the same vehicle in the same year. Choose the method that gives you the larger deduction — for most Uber drivers, the standard mileage rate wins.

California Uber Driver Tax FAQs 2026

What can California Uber drivers deduct in 2026?
Mileage at $0.725/mile, phone and data plan, car maintenance, tolls, parking, dash cam, car washes, and passenger supplies. California SDI is deductible if you opt in as self-employed via EDD.
Are California Uber drivers employees or contractors in 2026?
Under Prop 22 (2020), Uber drivers in California are independent contractors. You file Schedule C, pay your own SE tax (15.3%), and pay California state income tax on net profit.
Do California Uber drivers pay quarterly estimated taxes?
Yes — both federal (IRS Form 1040-ES) and California state (FTB Form 540-ES). Deadlines: Apr 15, Jun 16, Sep 15, Jan 15 2027.
How much tax does a California Uber driver pay in 2026?
On $35,000 net income: approximately $4,950 SE tax + $2,800 federal + $2,100 California state = ~$9,850 total. Mileage deductions significantly reduce this number.
What is the 1099-NEC threshold for Uber in 2026?
Under OBBBA 2026, the 1099-NEC threshold increased to $2,000. But you must report all self-employment income over $400 net profit regardless of whether you receive a 1099 form.
