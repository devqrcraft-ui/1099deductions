Uber Driver Taxes in Florida 2026: No State Tax + SE Tax Guide

Florida 2026 Updated May 2026 ~9 min read · 2,200 words
Quick Answer: Uber drivers in Florida owe $4,945 in federal SE tax on $35,000 net profit in 2026. Florida has no state income tax — no state return, no SDI, no state quarterly payments. Your only obligations are federal: SE tax at 15.3% and federal income tax. Quarterly estimated payment: $1,938 per quarter.

Key Takeaways

  • Florida has no state income tax — Uber drivers save $700–$2,200 vs high-tax states
  • Federal SE tax on $35K net profit: $4,945 (15.3% on 92.35% of income)
  • Quarterly estimated payment: $1,938 (federal only — no FL state payment)
  • IRS mileage rate: 72.5 cents/mile — 15,000 miles deducts $10,875 from taxable income

Does Florida Have a State Income Tax on Uber Driver Earnings?

No. Florida is one of nine US states with no personal income tax. As an Uber driver in Florida, you do not file a state income tax return, owe Florida state income tax, or make Florida quarterly estimated payments.

This is a major financial advantage. On $35,000 of Uber income, a Florida driver keeps $700–$2,200 more per year than drivers in states like California (up to 13.3%) or New York (up to 10.9%).

The Nine No-Tax States in 2026

Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, Wyoming. Florida Uber drivers owe federal tax only — no state return required.

"Florida does not impose a personal income tax on individuals. No state income tax return is required for Florida residents."

— Florida Department of Revenue, 2026

How Does Federal Self-Employment Tax Work for Uber Drivers in Florida?

Even without state tax, Florida Uber drivers owe federal self-employment (SE) tax. As an independent contractor, you pay both the employee and employer share of Social Security (12.4%) and Medicare (2.9%) — a total of 15.3%.

The IRS applies SE tax to 92.35% of your net profit. This 7.65% reduction accounts for the employer-equivalent deduction.

SE Tax Formula — $35,000 Uber Income

Step 1: $35,000 × 92.35% = $32,323 SE taxable base

Step 2: $32,323 × 15.3% = $4,945 SE tax

Quarterly payment: $4,945 ÷ 4 = ~$1,938 per quarter (before income tax portion)

You also deduct half of SE tax ($2,473) from your gross income before calculating federal income tax. This reduces your adjusted gross income and lowers your total tax bill.

Uber income is reported on 1099-K (if you received $5,000+ in payments) or 1099-NEC (for bonuses and referrals). Report all income on Schedule C regardless of whether you receive a 1099.

Here is the complete federal tax picture for a single Florida Uber driver earning $35,000 net profit with $10,875 in mileage deductions (15,000 miles at 72.5¢):

Without mileage deductions, SE tax on the full $35,000 would be $4,945 and quarterly payments approximately $1,938. Tracking 15,000 miles saves $1,536 in SE tax alone.

Important

What Deductions Can Florida Uber Drivers Claim?

Uber drivers file Schedule C with their federal return. Every legitimate business expense reduces your net profit — directly reducing SE tax and federal income tax.

Mileage — Biggest Deduction for Uber Drivers

The IRS standard mileage rate for 2026 is 72.5 cents per mile. Count every mile driven with the Uber app on — to pick up riders, during trips, and returning to your zone.

  • 10,000 miles = $7,250 deduction
  • 15,000 miles = $10,875 deduction
  • 20,000 miles = $14,500 deduction
  • 25,000 miles = $18,125 deduction

Phone and Data Plan

Deduct the business-use percentage of your phone bill. Most Uber drivers use their phone 70–90% for the app, navigation, and rider communication. Track this percentage and apply it to your monthly bill and device cost.

Water, Mints, and Rider Supplies

Any supplies you provide to riders — water bottles, phone chargers, mints, air fresheners — are deductible business supplies. Keep receipts.

Car Washes and Detailing

Car cleaning expenses are deductible at your business-use percentage. If you use your car 80% for Uber, deduct 80% of car wash and detailing costs.

Vehicle Expenses (Alternative Method)

Instead of the mileage rate, you can deduct actual expenses: gas, insurance, oil changes, tires, registration, and depreciation — prorated by business use. Calculate both methods to see which gives a higher deduction.

Half of SE Tax

Deduct 50% of your self-employment tax from gross income on Schedule 1. On $4,945 SE tax, that is a $2,473 deduction — taken automatically when filing Form 1040.

How to Pay Quarterly Estimated Taxes as a Florida Uber Driver

Uber does not withhold taxes. You must pay federal estimated taxes four times per year using IRS Form 1040-ES. Florida has no state quarterly tax requirement.

Quarter Income Period Due Date
Q1 2026 January – March April 15, 2026
Q2 2026 April – May June 16, 2026
Q3 2026 June – August September 15, 2026
Q4 2026 September – December January 15, 2027

For $35,000 annual income (no deductions), your estimated quarterly federal payment is approximately $1,938. Pay via IRS Direct Pay — free, instant, no account required.

  1. Go to IRS Direct Pay at irs.gov/payments
  2. Select "Estimated Tax" as the reason for payment
  3. Select "1040-ES" as the applicable tax form
  4. Enter tax year 2026 and your bank details
  5. Submit — save the confirmation number as proof
Florida Advantage — Simpler Quarterly Filing

Florida Uber drivers file only one quarterly estimated payment (federal 1040-ES). Drivers in California file two (federal + CA 540-ES). In New York, two (federal + NY IT-2105). Florida saves time and paperwork every quarter.

How to File Taxes as a Florida Uber Driver: Step-by-Step

Tax filing for Florida Uber drivers is simpler than most states — no state return, no state forms. Here is the full process:

  1. Download your Uber tax summary — available in the Uber driver app under Tax Info each January.
  2. Collect your 1099 forms — 1099-K for ride payments, 1099-NEC for bonuses. Report all income even without a form.
  3. Total all Uber income — fares, tips, surge pay, promotions, referral bonuses.
  4. Calculate your deductions — pull your mileage log, phone bills, supplies receipts.
  5. Complete Schedule C — enter gross income, subtract deductions, calculate net profit.
  6. Complete Schedule SE — calculate SE tax on net profit from Schedule C.
  7. File Form 1040 — attach Schedule C and Schedule SE. No Florida state return required.

"Independent contractors who receive payments of $600 or more must report that income on Schedule C. Estimated tax payments help avoid underpayment penalties."

— IRS Self-Employed Tax Center, IRS.gov

Frequently Asked Questions: Uber Driver Taxes in Florida 2026

How much SE tax does an Uber driver pay in Florida on $35,000?

On $35,000 net profit, an Uber driver in Florida owes $4,945 in self-employment tax (15.3% on 92.35% of income). Florida has no state income tax, so there is no state tax bill on top of that.

Does Florida have a state income tax on Uber driver earnings?

No. Florida is one of nine states with no state income tax. Uber drivers in Florida owe only federal self-employment tax and federal income tax — no Florida state return is required.

What mileage deduction can Florida Uber drivers claim in 2026?

The IRS standard mileage rate for 2026 is 72.5 cents per mile. A Florida Uber driver doing 15,000 miles can deduct $10,875 from federal taxable income, saving approximately $1,631 in SE tax.

When are Florida quarterly tax payments due for Uber drivers?

Federal quarterly estimated tax due dates in 2026: April 15, June 16, September 15, and January 15, 2027. Florida has no separate state estimated tax. File only federal Form 1040-ES each quarter.

Can Uber drivers in Florida deduct vehicle expenses?

Yes. Use the standard mileage rate (72.5 cents/mile) or deduct actual vehicle expenses — gas, insurance, repairs, depreciation — prorated by business use percentage. Most drivers find the mileage rate simpler and often higher.

Do Florida Uber drivers pay less tax than other states?

Yes. Because Florida has no state income tax, Uber drivers save $700–$2,200 per year compared to high-tax states like California or New York. Only federal SE tax and income tax apply.

Does Uber send a 1099 to Florida drivers?

Yes. Uber sends a 1099-K if you received $5,000+ in payments or a 1099-NEC for bonuses over $600. Even without a 1099, all Uber income must be reported on Schedule C of your federal return.
