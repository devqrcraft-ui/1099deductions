Airbnb Host Tax Deductions 2026 — Schedule E Write-Off Checklist

Airbnb hosts who rent their property for more than 14 days per year must report rental income — but can deduct a proportional share of property expenses on IRS Schedule E. If you also provide substantial services (cleaning, meals, concierge), income may be reported on Schedule C instead. This guide covers every major deduction available to Airbnb hosts in 2026.

Airbnb Host 1099 Tax FAQ

What is the Airbnb 14-day rule?
If you rent your property for 14 days or fewer per year, the rental income is completely tax-free under IRC Section 280A. Above 14 days, you must report income but can deduct expenses proportionally.
Does Airbnb send a 1099?
Yes. Airbnb issues a 1099-K if your gross payments exceed $5,000 in 2026. Even without a 1099, all rental income must be reported to the IRS.
Do Airbnb hosts pay self-employment tax?
Not for passive rental income reported on Schedule E. If you provide substantial services (hotel-like services), income shifts to Schedule C and becomes subject to SE tax 15.3%.
How do I calculate rental-use percentage?
Divide rental days by total days the property was used (rental + personal). Example: 180 rental days ÷ 240 total days = 75% rental use. Apply this percentage to shared expenses.
Can I deduct home improvements on Airbnb?
Improvements (adding value or extending life) must be depreciated over time — not immediately expensed. Repairs (maintaining current condition) are immediately deductible.
